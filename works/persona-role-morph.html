人格考古學 Archaeology of Persona

插畫立體化 Illustration to Sculpture

從色鉛筆插畫到手捏陶塑，將平面的內在形象轉化為可以被觀看、觸摸與環繞的立體存在。 From colored-pencil illustration into hand-built ceramic sculpture.

線條召喚 Line Summoning

線稿先召喚出角色的輪廓與氣息，再由陶土承接重量，讓內在形象從紙面走向立體。 Drawing first summons the contour and breath of a character; clay then receives its weight, allowing the inner image to move from paper into three dimensions.

將色鉛筆中由茶壺生長出的樹精幻象，轉譯為從壺中被召喚、噴湧而出的手捏陶塑；拉開樹精與壺的距離，保留枝幹的舞動、果實、眼睛與植物性，使平面的生命圖像化為在牆面與空間中延展的陶塑精靈。 A tree-spirit vision growing from a teapot in colored pencil is translated into a hand-built ceramic form summoned and surging from the vessel. The distance between spirit and teapot is opened while the dancing branches, fruit, eyes, and vegetal energy are preserved, turning a flat image of life into a ceramic spirit extending across wall and space.

面孔浮雕 Reliefs of the Face

這一段聚焦臉與凝視：線條中的眼睛、鼻與嘴，在陶塑中被加深成厚度、陰影與精神重量。 This section focuses on face and gaze: the eyes, nose, and mouth in the lines are deepened in ceramic form into thickness, shadow, and spiritual weight.

非表象系列 Nonrepresentational Series

非表象系列把自我、原貌、延伸與存有拆成多個陶土證據，使人格不以單一肖像出現，而以複合形體顯影。 The Nonrepresentational Series breaks self, original form, extension, and being into multiple clay evidences, allowing persona to appear not as a single portrait but as composite form.

將《非表象之存有》中既是樹也是自我的內在形象，轉譯為可環繞觀看的陶塑《複合之身》；彩色葉髮、誇張鼻唇、樹枝般的手臂與透視之眼，從平面人格圖像進化為複合生命的立體存在。 The inner image in Non-Representational Being, both tree and self, is translated into the walk-around ceramic work Composite Body. Colored leaf-like hair, exaggerated nose and lips, branch-like arms, and a penetrating eye evolve from a flat persona image into a three-dimensional composite life.

開啟插畫原圖 Open Original Drawing 開啟陶塑照片 Open Ceramic Photo 觀看完整《複合之身》 View Complete Body of Many Spirits

插畫立體化讓線條不再停留在紙面，而成為可以投下陰影、承受重量、被人繞行觀看的陶塑身體。 Illustration transformed into sculpture lets line leave the page and become a ceramic body that casts shadows, carries weight, and can be viewed in the round.

在平面與立體之間，人格像被一層層翻出來，既保留線稿的自由，也長出陶土的存在感。 Between flat image and three-dimensional form, persona seems to be turned outward layer by layer, retaining the freedom of drawing while growing the presence of clay.
