陶與生命容器 Ceramic Beings

陶土在這個系列中不只是器皿，也像生命暫住的身體。眼睛、嘴巴、孔洞、花與痕跡彼此牽引，使每件作品都成為一個會觀看、會呼吸，也會保存記憶的容器。 In this series, clay is not only a vessel; it is a temporary body for life. Eyes, mouths, openings, flowers, and traces pull one another into works that gaze, breathe, and preserve memory.

心生相 Inner Form

作為《陶與生命容器系列》的第一件作品，《心生相》像是整個系列最初的入口，讓內在形象從陶土、孔洞與器形之間慢慢顯現。 As the first work in Clay and Vessels of Life, Inner Form becomes the series' first threshold, where inner images slowly appear through clay, openings, and vessel-like form.

從器物看見生命如何停駐 Seeing How Life Dwells in Vessels

這個系列可以先從五件代表作品進入：從最初的《心生相》，到拒絕被功能命名的《非花器》，再走向凝視、祭儀與回望。它們讓陶土不只是容器，而是保存眼神、呼吸與記憶的生命場所。 The series may be entered through five representative works: from the first Inner Form to Not a Vase, which resists functional naming, then onward to gaze, ritual, and return. Together they make clay not merely a container, but a living place for gaze, breath, and memory.

凝視與訊號 Gaze and Signals

眼睛、表情與不可言說的訊號在這裡浮現，使觀看不再只是外部動作，而變成作品與觀者之間的相互回應。 Eyes, expressions, and unspeakable signals emerge here, turning viewing from an external act into a mutual response between work and viewer.

三件一組的被視之物，像三個被看見的瞬間；它們彼此靠近，卻各自保留自己的沉默。 This group of three beheld objects feels like three moments of being seen. They stand close together, yet each keeps its own silence.

陶與生命容器把陶土從器物推向生命：它們有孔洞、有眼睛、有嘴巴，也有可以保存情緒與記憶的內部空間。 Clay and Vessels of Life pushes clay from object toward life. These works have openings, eyes, mouths, and inner spaces that preserve emotion and memory.

當觀看在作品之間往返，容器不再只是盛放物品，而像一群安靜的生命，替那些尚未說出口的感受留下形體。 As viewing moves among the works, vessels no longer merely hold objects. They become quiet lives, giving form to feelings not yet spoken.
